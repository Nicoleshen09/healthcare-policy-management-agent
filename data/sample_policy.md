# Coverage Policy CP-2041: Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGM)

## Section 1. Purpose
This policy defines the coverage criteria used to adjudicate claims for therapeutic continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) devices billed under procedure code E2103. It is a synthetic document created for demonstration purposes and does not represent any real payer or CMS policy.

## Section 2. Covered Indications
A CGM device (E2103) is covered when the member has a documented diagnosis of diabetes mellitus. Covered diagnoses include ICD-10 codes in the E10 family (type 1 diabetes) and the E11 family (type 2 diabetes). The member must be under an active insulin treatment regimen or have a documented history of problematic hypoglycemia.

## Section 3. Frequency Limitations
Coverage for a replacement CGM device is limited to one device per member per twelve (12) month period. A claim for a replacement device submitted fewer than 365 days after the most recent covered device will not be paid unless a documented exception (loss, theft, or irreparable damage) is on file.

## Section 4. Prior Authorization
Prior authorization is required for all initial CGM device claims. Claims submitted without prior authorization on file are routed to manual review and are not automatically payable.

## Section 5. Exclusions
CGM devices are not covered for members without a qualifying diabetes diagnosis. Devices billed for wellness, screening, or general monitoring without an eligible E10 or E11 diagnosis are excluded from coverage.
